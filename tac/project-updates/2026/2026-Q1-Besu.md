# 2026 Q1 Besu

## Project Health

Besu had a strong Q1 2026, with accelerating development activity focused on the upcoming Glamsterdam Ethereum network upgrade and continued performance optimization work. The project shipped two releases, merged 320 PRs from 33 unique contributors, and made significant progress on technical debt removal including the full removal of ETC Classic support, Fast Sync, and Clique block production. Three maintainers moved to emeritus status, bringing the active count to 18.

### Major Accomplishments This Quarter

**Glamsterdam Protocol Development**: Heavy implementation work for the next Ethereum network upgrade, with 15+ PRs focused on EIP-7928 Block Access Lists (BAL) including validation, size caps, uniqueness constraints, BAL metrics, chain pruning integration, and devnet-3 readiness. Additional EIPs implemented include EIP-8037 (State Creation Gas Cost Increase), EIP-8024 (opcode changes), EIP-7954, EIP-7778, EIP-7843 (SLOTNUM opcode), EIP-7708 (SELFDESTRUCT changes), EIP-7975 (eth/70 partial receipts), EIP-8159 (eth/71 BAL exchange), and EIP-8189 (snap/2 protocol).

**Performance Optimizations**: ADD opcode 86% faster via new UInt256 implementation, EIP-196 AltBn128 EcAdd 33-128% faster and EcMul 8% faster, engine_getBlobsV2 up to 10x throughput improvement by disabling HTTP compression for engine API, BytesTrieSet replaced with HashSet (48% improvement for CREATE/CREATE2/SELFDESTRUCT), parallelized Bonsai state root computation, EVM optimizations improving 70% of EEST benchmarks, and shift opcode (SAR/SHR/SHL) improvements.

**Tech Debt Removal**: Completed removal of ETC Classic and Mordor network support, removed Fast Sync and deprecated CHECKPOINT sync, removed Clique RPC APIs and block production infrastructure, removed pre-eth/68 transaction announcement support, and removed Forest DB backup/restore subcommands. Over 20 cleanup PRs addressed dead code and redundant logic throughout the codebase.

**DiscV5 and IPv6**: Major investment in next-generation peer discovery with IPv4/IPv6 dual-stack support for UDP discovery and RLPx TCP binding, static bootnode support, ENR v5 bootnodes, PeerPermissions and subnet filtering integration, and discovery metrics. New CLI options for IPv6 configuration.

### Quarterly Release Summary

During Q1 2026, the Besu project released:

- *Besu 26.1.0* (February): Breaking removal of `--Xenable-extra-debug-tracers` flag, `engine_getBlobsV3` method, EIP-7778 Amsterdam support, major performance improvements including ADD opcode 86% faster and getBlobsV2 10x throughput
- *Besu 26.2.0* (March): Full removal of ETC Classic/Mordor support, redesigned chain pruning CLI with three strategies (ALL, BAL, NONE), removal of Fast Sync, IPv6 dual-stack support for DiscV5, `4byteTracer` in debug_trace methods, and EVM optimizations improving 70% of EEST benchmarks

## Project Adoption and Usage

### Public Network Usage

Besu holds approximately 10-16% of mainnet execution layer stake, maintaining its role as the third most popular Ethereum execution client behind Geth and Nethermind. Institutional confidence remains strong, with major staking providers including Besu in their multi-client infrastructure.

### Enterprise and Private Networks

**Financial Services**: Continued adoption in financial services, with institutions such as Citi leveraging Besu's QBFT consensus and enterprise features.

**Layer 2 Integration**: Continued presence in Layer 2 scaling solutions, with Besu serving as execution layer infrastructure for rollup projects including Linea. Development of plugin APIs to support additional L2 implementations.

**Private Networks**: Ongoing adoption for permissioned networks leveraging QBFT consensus, with improvements to IBFT2-to-QBFT migration and enterprise plugin APIs.
