# 2025 Q4 Besu

## Project Health

Besu demonstrated exceptional project health in Q4 2025, achieving a landmark milestone with the successful increase of Ethereum mainnet's block gas limit from 30 million to 60 million. The quarter was marked by three major releases supporting critical network upgrades, continued performance optimization efforts, and strategic cleanup of deprecated features. The team's focus on the Fusaka hard fork showcases Besu's commitment to maintaining compatibility with evolving Ethereum specifications while pushing the boundaries of execution client performance.

### Major Accomplishments This Quarter

**Gas Limit Expansion to 60 Million**: The culmination of year-long optimization efforts, Besu successfully supported Ethereum mainnet's increase to 60 million gas per block in Q4 2025. This achievement required extensive optimization of opcodes and precompiles to ensure worst-case scenarios could be processed within the 3-second block window, representing a doubling of network capacity while maintaining reliability and performance standards.

**Fusaka and Multi-Fork Support**: Q4 saw Besu deliver mainnet-ready support for the Fusaka hard fork along with subsequent BPO1, and BPO2 upgrades. Version 25.11.0 provided the mandatory update for mainnet users, with fork activation times scheduled through early 2026 (Fusaka: December 3, 2025; BPO1: December 9, 2025; BPO2: January 7, 2026).

**Sync Performance Enhancements**: Significant improvements to synchronization speed were achieved by eliminating unnecessary RLP decoding of block bodies during sync, reducing memory and CPU usage while increasing download pipeline parallelism from 4 to 8 concurrent operations. These optimizations substantially reduced mainnet sync times for new node operators.

**Architectural Cleanup and Modernization**: The quarter included strategic removal of deprecated Proof of Work features, legacy CLI options, and outdated code paths. This cleanup effort improved maintainability while signaling Besu's focus on post-merge Ethereum operations and enterprise-grade features for modern networks.

### Quarterly Release Summary

During Q4 2025, the Besu project released:

- **Besu 25.10.0** (October 16): Fusaka testnet support with optional sender balance checks
- **Besu 25.11.0** (November 5): Mandatory Fusaka mainnet release with 60M gas limit and multi-fork support
- **Besu 25.12.0** (December 12): Enhanced RPC compatibility and experimental call tracing

These releases included over 80 merged pull requests focusing on protocol upgrades, performance optimization, architectural improvements, and developer experience enhancements.

## Releases

### Major Releases (Q4 2025)

**25.12.0** - December 12, 2025
- Removed deprecated CLI options including `--Xbonsai-parallel-tx-processing-enabled` and all Proof of Work related flags
- Enhanced RPC compatibility with execution specification for error codes and revert handling
- 5-6x performance improvement for `engine_getBlobsV2` through optimized toFastHex calculation
- Added `eth_subscribe` and `eth_unsubscribe` support to IPC service
- Introduced experimental `callTracer` option for debug tracing methods
- Upgraded to Netty 4.2.9.Final and integrated Eclipse Temurin OpenJDK JRE in Docker images
- Added Linea named network support (mainnet and sepolia)
- Breaking change: Removed PoW-specific RPCs (`eth_getMinerDataByBlockHash`, `miner_setCoinbase`, etc.)

**25.11.0** - November 5, 2025
- Mandatory mainnet update for Fusaka hard fork support
- Added Osaka, BPO1, and BPO2 fork times for Ethereum mainnet
- Increased mainnet gas limit to 60 million (up from 30 million)
- Added `blockTimestamp` field to receipt logs for enhanced transaction data retrieval
- Upgraded execution-spec-tests to v5.3.0 for improved protocol compliance testing
- Enhanced debug tracing with ability to enable/disable stack, storage, and returnData independently
- Updated Netty to 4.2.7.Final and Tuweni to 2.7.2

**25.10.0** - October 16, 2025
- Recommended update for Holesky, Sepolia, and Hoodi testnet users for Fusaka
- Implemented optional sender balance checks in layered transaction pool
- Added `--cache-last-block-headers` flag for improved header caching during non-finality conditions
- Performance improvements for MOD-related opcodes (MOD, ADDMOD, MULMOD)
- Added `blockTimestamp` to `eth_getLogs` results
- Introduced `--ethstats-report-interval` CLI option for configurable EthStats reporting
- Fixed eth_subscribe RPC failures and ethstats integration issues

### Point Releases and Patches

## Overall Activity in the Past Quarter

### Community Engagement

**GitHub Activity**: The project maintained high development velocity with over 80 merged pull requests during Q4, including contributions from both core maintainers and community members. Notable community engagement around performance testing and gas limit validation helped ensure stable mainnet operations.

**Cross-Client Collaboration**: Active participation in Ethereum All Core Devs discussions around Fusaka. Coordination with other execution clients ensured consistent behavior across the network during critical hard fork activations. Performance improvements in particular were heavily collaborative with other client teams.

**Developer Experience**: Continued improvements to debugging and tracing capabilities with the addition of experimental `callTracer` support and enhanced control over trace output verbosity. Documentation updates accompanied deprecated feature removal to guide operators through migration paths.

### Technical Development

**Performance Optimization Initiative**: Q4 represented the culmination of the year-long performance roadmap:
- Opcode optimizations: EQ, MOD, ADDMOD, MULMOD, CallDataCopy, ExtCodeHash
- Precompile enhancements: EcRecover and P256Verify performance improvements
- Sync optimization through minimal RLP decoding and increased parallelism
- Global code caching and parallel transaction processing enabled by default

**Protocol Development**: Active implementation of multiple protocol upgrades:
- Fusaka hard fork: Complete implementation and mainnet deployment
- Osaka, BPO1, BPO2: Preparation and configuration for upcoming activations
- EIP-7910: Implementation of `eth_config` JSON-RPC method for devnets
- Amsterdam blob schedule: Integration work for future upgrades

**Enterprise and Private Network Features**: Continued enhancements for enterprise deployments:
- QBFT consensus improvements with better handling of transaction selection timeouts
- Support for Linea networks with dedicated named network configurations
- Enhanced plugin system with configuration reload capabilities
- Improved eth_simulate error handling and gas estimation accuracy

### Security and Stability

**Dependency Updates**: Regular security-focused dependency updates including Netty (4.2.9.Final), Vertx (4.5.22), and Tuweni (2.7.2) ensuring the latest security patches are incorporated.

**Breaking Changes with Migration Paths**: Strategic removal of deprecated features with advance notice and clear migration documentation. No critical vulnerabilities reported in production releases during Q4.

**Test Coverage**: Upgraded to execution-spec-tests v5.3.0, maintaining comprehensive protocol compliance validation and catching edge cases before production deployment.

## Project Adoption and Usage

### Public Network Usage

Besu maintains its critical role in Ethereum's client diversity, continuing to hold approximately 15-17% of mainnet stake. The successful support of the gas limit increase to 60 million demonstrated Besu's readiness for high-throughput mainnet operations, reinforcing confidence among institutional staking providers.

**Validator Confidence**: The mandatory 25.11.0 upgrade for Fusaka saw strong adoption rates among validators, with minimal disruption during hard fork activation. Major staking providers maintained multi-client strategies with Besu as a key component of their infrastructure.

### Enterprise and Private Networks

**Financial Services Adoption**: Continued growth in financial services deployments, with institutions leveraging Besu's enterprise features including QBFT consensus, permissioning capabilities, and enhanced operational tooling for production systems.

**Layer 2 Ecosystem**: Expanding presence in Layer 2 infrastructure, evidenced by the addition of dedicated Linea network support in 25.12.0. Besu serves as the execution layer for various rollup solutions, benefiting from performance optimizations that improve L2 settlement efficiency.

**Private Network Deployments**: Strong uptake of QBFT consensus improvements for permissioned networks, with enhanced transaction pool management and block creation reliability supporting production enterprise use cases.

**Development and Testing Networks**: Active usage across Ethereum testnets (Holesky, Sepolia, Hoodi) with timely support for protocol upgrades, enabling developers to test applications against upcoming mainnet changes.
